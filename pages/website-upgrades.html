The Problem

Most local business websites were built years ago and haven't been touched since.

They load slowly, look outdated on mobile, bury the phone number, have weak calls to action, and don't give first-time visitors a reason to trust the business.

The fix is usually not a full rebuild — it's targeted improvements to the parts that matter most. I specialize in finding those specific problems and fixing them — fast, clearly scoped, and at a price that makes sense for a local business.

"I noticed a few things on your site that I think could be cleaned up pretty quickly to make it feel more polished and convert better."

— What I say in my first message to a new prospect

I don't open with "AI." I open with specific, honest observations about your actual site.

The Audit

What I Look For

I review only what's publicly visible — no access to your backend required.

Weak or missing homepage headline — visitors can't tell what you do in 5 seconds
No clear call to action above the fold — people don't know what to do next
Phone number buried or missing — especially damaging on mobile
Cluttered navigation — too many options, no clear priority
Weak or absent trust signals — no reviews, credentials, or real photos
Generic stock photos — makes the site feel impersonal and untrustworthy
Services poorly described — no explanation of who they're for or how to get started
Broken or confusing contact forms — the single biggest lead-loss point
Poor mobile experience — layout breaks, text too small, buttons too small to tap
Outdated content — old promotions, wrong hours, stale blog posts
Weak local SEO basics — wrong page title, no location in meta
Inconsistent contact info — doesn't match Google Business listing
