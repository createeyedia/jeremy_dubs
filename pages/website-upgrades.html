The Audit

What I Look For

I review only what's publicly visible — no access to your backend required. Here's what I check:

Weak or missing homepage headline — visitors can't tell what you do in 5 seconds

No clear call to action above the fold — people don't know what to do next

Phone number buried or missing — especially damaging on mobile

Cluttered navigation — too many options, no clear priority

Weak or absent trust signals — no reviews, credentials, or real photos

Generic stock photos — makes the site feel impersonal and untrustworthy

Services poorly described — no explanation of who they're for or how to get started

Broken or confusing contact forms — the single biggest lead-loss point

Outdated content — old promotions, wrong hours, stale blog posts from 2019

Poor mobile experience — layout breaks, text too small, buttons too small to tap

Weak local SEO basics — wrong page title, no location in meta, unverified Google Business

Inconsistent contact info — address or phone doesn't match Google Business listing

FAQ

Common Questions

Do I need a full rebuild?+
Usually not. Most local business sites have specific problems — a weak headline, a broken form, a cluttered layout — that can be fixed without starting over. I'll tell you honestly if I think a rebuild is genuinely the better option, but in most cases it isn't.
What platform does my site need to be on?+
I work with WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, Webflow, and custom HTML/CSS sites. If you're not sure what your site is built on, I can figure that out during the audit.
How do you handle payment?+
50% upfront before work begins, 50% on delivery. I use a simple invoice — no complicated contracts for smaller projects.
What if I already have a web person?+
That's worth knowing upfront. If you have an active web person maintaining your site, this service may not be the right fit — I'd rather tell you that now than waste your time. If your web person built the site but doesn't actively maintain it, there's often a good opportunity to improve what's there.
Do you offer video or photography as part of the upgrade?+
Yes — and this is where the combination gets powerful. A website rewrite can only do so much if the photos are generic stock images. If your site needs real photography or video content to actually work, I can provide that too. It's priced separately but built into the same engagement so everything is consistent.
How is this different from an SEO agency?+
SEO agencies typically focus on content volume, backlinks, and keyword rankings over months. I focus on fixing what a real human visitor experiences when they land on your site — and making sure the basics of local SEO (page titles, Google Business alignment, consistent NAP data) are correct. Those fundamentals often matter more for local businesses than elaborate SEO campaigns.
