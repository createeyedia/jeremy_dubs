Video Production — Anniversary Film

NW Montessori School — 60th Anniversary

Seattle, WA
Creative Direction + Editing
Ongoing Partnership
Anniversary Film Archival Footage Phone-Recorded Testimonials Interviews Fundraiser Screening Ongoing Production

The Project

Northwest Montessori School came to us for their 60th anniversary fundraiser. They needed a video that captured the heart of the school's legacy — its people, philosophy, and sense of community — while being concise and impactful enough for a live event setting.

The challenge: telling a 60-year story in 90 seconds, with limited time and a need to involve voices from across the school's entire history. We designed a hybrid production approach that balanced emotional depth with practical constraints.

The Approach

Instead of relying solely on traditional filming, we guided current and former students, teachers, and administrators to record personal reflections on their phones. We conducted key interviews with leadership — including the Head of School, Elizabeth, and former director, Jan. Then we wove in archival photos and school history to connect past and present.

The narrative was built around a theme: "Rooted in Legacy, Growing Our Future."

What We Delivered

  • 90-second cinematic teaser optimized for live event presentation
  • Creative direction and story development across three arcs: legacy, community, future
  • Guided phone-recorded reflections from students, alumni, and staff
  • Key interviews with school leadership
  • Archival photo animation (Ken Burns) with clean transitions and warm grading
  • Selected and refined impactful soundbites from multiple contributors
  • Scalable foundation for a future long-form documentary version
  • Ongoing monthly community spotlight videos

Creative Direction

We shaped the story around three ideas: the legacy of founder Marietta and the "Expect a Miracle" cards, the sense of community as home, and the inspiration for continued support. The 90-second film was designed to resonate emotionally during the fundraiser while opening the door for a deeper, more expansive story down the line.
